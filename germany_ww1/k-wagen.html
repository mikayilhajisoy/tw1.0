K-wagen

The K-Wagen was a massive German super-heavy tank developed in the final year of World War I, intended to break through fortified Allied trench systems. Conceived in 1917 and designed by Joseph Vollmer, it was far larger than the earlier A7V. The K-Wagen measured about 13 meters long, 6 meters wide, and 3 meters tall, with an estimated combat weight of around 120 tons. Its huge size made it one of the heaviest armored vehicles ever attempted during the war. The K-Wagen was powered by two Daimler engines with a combined output of about 650 horsepower, but given its immense weight, the speed would have been only 5–8 km/h at best. Two prototypes were under construction by 1918, but neither was completed before the Armistice. Both were dismantled after the war under Allied supervision. None survive today, leaving the K-Wagen as a symbol of unrealized German tank ambitions.

    Characteristics:
  • Type: Super-Heavy tank
  • Weight: 120 tons
  • Crew: 27
  • Armament: 4 x 77 mm guns; 7 x 7.92mm MG08 machine guns
  • Speed: 8 km/h
