Whippet Mark A

The Medium Mark A, nicknamed the “Whippet,” was Britain’s first medium tank, introduced in 1917 as a complement to the heavy rhomboid-shaped Mark series. While the heavy tanks were designed to break through trench lines, the Whippet was intended to exploit breakthroughs with greater speed and mobility. The Whippet first saw combat in 1917 and proved especially effective in open warfare during the German Spring Offensive and the Allied Hundred Days Offensive in 1918. Its speed allowed it to harass enemy infantry, machine gun nests, and artillery positions. Although vulnerable to artillery and armor-piercing fire, the Whippet demonstrated the value of faster, lighter tanks and influenced future armored warfare concepts, including cavalry-style exploitation units.
    Characteristics:
  • Type: Medium tank
  • Weight: 14 tons
  • Crew: 3
  • Armament: 4 x 0.303 inch Hotchkiss machine guns
  • Speed: 13 km/h
