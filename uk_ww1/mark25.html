Improved Mark variants

The British Mark IV and Mark V represented the evolution of the first tanks into more effective battlefield machines. Introduced in 1917, the Mark IV was the first to be produced in large numbers, with over 1,200 built. It featured thicker armor (up to 12 mm) and came in “Male” (6-pounder guns + machine guns) and “Female” (only machine guns) versions. It proved its value at Cambrai in 1917, where tanks were used in mass formation for the first time, though mechanical breakdowns and artillery still took a heavy toll. Together, the Mark IV and V showed how quickly tank technology developed during the war. They demonstrated that armored vehicles could support infantry in large-scale operations and laid the foundation for interwar tank designs.
