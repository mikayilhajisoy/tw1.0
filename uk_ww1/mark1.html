Mark 1

The Mark I was the world’s first combat tank, deployed by Britain at the Battle of the Somme in September 1916. Its distinctive rhomboid shape, with tracks running around the entire hull, allowed it to cross trenches up to 3 meters wide and barbed wire, tasks that had previously been impossible for infantry and cavalry. Though mechanically unreliable, the Mark I shocked German forces and proved that armored vehicles could overcome trench warfare. Its success led to further development of heavier and more reliable designs.

    Characteristics:
  • Type: Heavy tank
  • Weight: 28 tons
  • Crew: 8
  • Armament: 2 x 6-pounder guns/ 3 x 0.303 inch machine guns
  • Speed: 6 km/h
