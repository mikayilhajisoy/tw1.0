M4A1 Sherman

The M4 Sherman was the primary medium tank of the United States and its Allies during World War II. Designed in 1941 to replace the M3 Lee, the Sherman became one of the most iconic and widely produced tanks of the war, with over 49,000 units built across many variants. It was valued for its reliability, mobility, and ease of mass production, though it often faced stronger German tanks like the Panther and Tiger. Although it was not the most powerful tank, the Sherman’s strengths were its mechanical reliability, large production numbers, and crew survivability. It served on all major fronts — Europe, North Africa, and the Pacific — and was used by many Allied nations. Postwar, it remained in service for decades worldwide, influencing future tank design.

    Characteristics:
  • Type: Medium tank
  • Weight: 30-35 tons
  • Crew: 5
  • Armament: 75mm M3, 76mm M1A1; 1 x .50 cal M2 Browning; 2 x .30 cal M1919
  • Speed: 40 km/h
