Mendeleev

The Mendeleev Tank was a conceptual Russian heavy tank designed in 1916 by engineer Vasily Mendeleev, son of the famous chemist Dmitri Mendeleev. It was an extremely ambitious design, intended to provide Russia with a breakthrough armored vehicle capable of dominating enemy fortifications. Unlike contemporary French and British tanks, the Mendeleev Tank prioritized massive firepower and heavy armor over mobility. The Mendeleev Tank never progressed beyond blueprints and technical plans, as Russia lacked the industrial capacity to produce such a colossal vehicle during World War I. It remained a visionary but impractical idea, reflecting both Russia’s late entry into tank development and the extreme ambitions of some designers. The project is remembered as one of the most ambitious and impractical tank proposals of the First World War.

    Characteristics:
  • Type: Super-Heavy tank
  • Weight: 173 tons
  • Crew: 8
  • Armament: 120mm Canet naval gun; 7.62mm Maxim machine gun
  • Speed: 6 km/h
