Tsar tank

The Tsar Tank, also called the Netopyr (“Bat”), was Russia’s most ambitious armored vehicle project during World War I, designed in 1914–1915 by Nikolai Lebedenko and a team of engineers. Unlike conventional tracked tanks, the Tsar Tank featured a radical tricycle design: two enormous front wheels about 9 meters in diameter and a much smaller rear wheel for balance. The huge wheels were intended to roll over trenches, barbed wire, and other battlefield obstacles. Only one prototype was ever built and tested, and it proved impractical on real terrain. By 1916, the project was abandoned, and the prototype was eventually scrapped. Although it never saw combat, the Tsar Tank remains a symbol of early Russian innovation and eccentricity in armored warfare, representing one of the most unusual and visually striking tank designs in history.

  • Weight: 60 tons

  • Armament: 2 x 76.2mm guns; 3 x 7.62mm machine guns
